Samples for the paper "Prompt-to-Speech Using a Large Language Model"

Discourse neutral task

We first prompt the model to make only local modifications based on the target text for a discourse neutral task. We measure perceived naturalness for this task to evaluate whether the local modifications, in particular, have a measurable impact on perceived naturalness. We compare the proposed method with the baseline model and the two reference-based models and use validation utterances from the LJSpeech corpus.

Target speaking style task

We measure the perceived "appropriateness" of utterances to evaluate the proposed method in predicting prosody for a given target speaking style. Participants are shown the target text and speaking style when performing this evaluation. We create a small text corpus for this where we first select a number speaking styles and, for each one, then generate a set of target texts that we deem would be appropriate for that style.

Dialogue task.

We also evaluate the appropriateness of utterances in the context of a two-line dialogue. Here, the LLM is instructed to make modifications based only on the previous line in a dialogue and the target text, not a target style. We first choose six "hidden" speaking styles and then generate 10 two-line dialogues where the target text would naturally fit the speaking style.

Example prompt

The following is an example prompt that was used to adjust duration, pitch and energy of a text given a target speaking style:

Prompt:

        
I am generating speech but I want to make it more prosodically appropriate for a target speaking style.
Assume that the speech that I currently have sounds as if spoken in a neutral discourse.
I want to change the pitch, energy and duration of the speech that I have synthesised to fit the text better.

I want you to make the following types of changes to the pitch, energy and duration of my synthesised speech:

1. Utterance-level changes: You can increase or decrease pitch, energy and duration for the whole utterance to
convey emotions and expressions. For each of pitch, energy and duration, you should pick a value between -5 and 5:
    * A negative value will decrease utterance-level pitch, energy or duration and a value of -5
      will decrease it as much as possible.
    * A positive value will increase utterance-level pitch, energy or duration and a value of 5
      will increase it as much as possible.
    * A value of 0 will leave the value as is.
(Note that increasing duration will make the speaker sound slower.)

2. Word-level changes: You can increase pitch, energy and duration for a specific word to emphasise it or
arbitrarily as you see fit. For each of pitch, energy and duration, you should pick a value between 0 and 5:
    * A value of 0 will leave the value as is.
    * A value higher than that will increase the word-level pitch, energy or duration.
      The higher the value, the more it increases.

Rules:
1. You should make your predictions without considering who the speaker is.
2. You have to follow the formatting instructions below.
3. You should provide reasoning for your suggestions at the end of your response.
4. You have to make word-level suggestions for every word in the target text.
5. You don't have to make suggest changes for punctuation symbols or spaces.
6. Your suggestions have to make sense. For example you can't recommend a negative value
   for utterance-level pitch but then say in your reasoning that pitch should be increased.

Formatting instructions:
Provide your utterance-level changes in a table like the one below:
|Pitch|Energy|Duration|
|---|---|---|
|your suggested pitch change|your suggested energy change|your suggested duration change|

Provide your word-level changes in a table like the one below:
|Word|Pitch|Energy|Duration|
|---|---|---|---|
|word 1|suggested pitch change for word 1|suggested energy change for word 1|suggested duration change for word 1|
|word 2|suggested pitch change for word 2|suggested energy change for word 2|suggested duration change for word 2|
...

Your first example is the following:
Target style: Talking to a child
Target text: I will always take care of you!

Response:

        
Here are my suggested prosodic changes for the given target text in the style of talking to a child:

Pitch	Energy	Duration
2	2	1

Word	Pitch	Energy	Duration
I	0	1	0
will	0	0	0
always	1	1	0
take	0	0	0
care	0	1	1
of	0	0	0
you	2	2	1

Just a slight increase in pitch, energy and duration is recommended to make the speech sound
more cheerful and caring.

For word-level changes, the word "always" should be emphasised by a slight increase in pitch
and energy to indicate that the speaker is making a promise to the child. The word "care"
should have a higher energy and longer duration to make the speaker sound more caring and
empathetic towards the child. The word "you" should have a significant increase in pitch,
energy and duration to make the child feel loved and appreciated.
